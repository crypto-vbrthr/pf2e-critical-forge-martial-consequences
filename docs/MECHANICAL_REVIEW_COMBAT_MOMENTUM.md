## Review Scope

The review examined schema validity, startup behavior, rules clarity, mechanical uniqueness, overlap within Combat Momentum and with Core, Arsenal, and Martial Openings, relative power, timing, action economy, compatibility with melee weapon, unarmed, natural, and out-of-turn Strikes, and table usability.

## Complete Redesigns

The following cards were redesigned:

- CM-002 Guard on the Return
- CM-012 Hands Still Moving
- CM-016 Center of the Fight
- CM-018 Adrenaline Dividend
- CM-019 Through the Gap
- CM-026 Close-Quarters Clarity

The revisions removed direct self-targeted versions of Martial Openings cards, a Core-style temporary-Hit-Point reward, retroactive reaction protection during Tumble Through, and repeated Seek or Recall Knowledge choices. They now use circumstance neutralization, a hand-independent combat maneuver, reach-based difficult terrain, a target-specific fortune effect, reaction protection from the beginning of Tumble Through, and a Step-triggered reaction window.

## Targeted Adjustments

- CM-003 now chooses a different enemy when the benefit is used and remains available through the end of the next turn.
- CM-004, CM-014, and CM-015 remain available through the end of the next turn.
- CM-005 is consumed only by a melee Strike whose multiple attack penalty it actually reduces.
- CM-006 is declared only for an action that could trigger a reaction from the target.
- CM-021 is rated Strong.
- CM-022 grants +2 AC when the target is between the source and a ranged attacker, with a +1 fallback otherwise.
- CM-023 explicitly preserves every normal stance rule and restriction.
- CM-024 explicitly preserves all reaction triggers, requirements, restrictions, and frequency limits.
- CM-025 removes restrained from the immediate branch and uses an immediate Step as a reliable fallback.
- CM-030 applies a -5 multiple attack penalty, or -4 for an agile attack, when the triggering critical Strike occurred outside the source's turn.
