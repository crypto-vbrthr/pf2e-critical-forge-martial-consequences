## Review Scope

The review examined schema validity, startup behavior, rules clarity, mechanical uniqueness, overlap within Martial Openings and with the other Martial Consequences packs, relative power, timing, action economy, compatibility with weapon, unarmed, and natural Strikes, and table usability.

## Complete Redesigns

The following cards were redesigned:

- MO-004 A Choice, Technically
- MO-007 No Safe Retreat
- MO-022 Passing the Breach
- MO-024 Instant Coordination
- MO-027 Pinned Between Threats

The revisions removed a choice that could benefit the target, a pursuit reaction unable to follow a normal Stride, two repeated follow-up Step or fixed attack-bonus patterns, and a Step-only containment restriction. They now use prepared Aid, half-Speed pursuit, chained reaction denial, coordinated protection from reactions, and a Grapple-or-Trip containment response.

## Targeted Adjustments

- MO-005 now applies its circumstance bonus explicitly to the relevant check.
- MO-010 is rated Light because its Take Cover and Hide opportunity remains terrain-dependent.
- MO-013 is consumed only by an eligible action that actually takes a multiple attack penalty.
- MO-017 now uses rules-aligned wording for being off-guard due to flanking.
- MO-019 gains an Interact fallback if its reaction trigger never occurs.
- MO-020 has normal draw weight and is consumed only when it removes a concealment flat check.
- MO-021 gains reaction denial as a fallback when no defensive circumstance bonus applies.
- MO-023 is defined as one simultaneous special movement effect rather than two ambiguous actions.
- MO-026 gains Take Cover and Drop Prone fallbacks when no shield or parry option is available.

## Result

**Mechanical Review: Passed after revision**

The pack now retains its strong teamwork identity while reducing repeated Step and fixed attack-bonus patterns. Its revised cards emphasize coordinated Aid, pursuit, reaction control, reliable defensive fallbacks, and active containment.
